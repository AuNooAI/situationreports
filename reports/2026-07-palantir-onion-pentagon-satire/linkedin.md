New Aunoo situation report on a satirical headline that was briefly read as a market signal.

Around 30 June, The Onion published "Palantir Acquires Pentagon For $800 Billion." The article reached the front page of r/wallstreetbets with roughly 24,000 upvotes, and users there attributed a same-day move in Palantir stock to automated trading systems that had indexed the Reddit reaction without recognising the source as satire.

In Aunoo's assessment, two things are confirmed and one is not. The Onion piece is satire, and the Reddit amplification happened. The claim at the centre of the story, that trading bots caused a price move, is so far unverified. It originates as user conjecture, and we have seen no market data or trading-firm statement that supports it. We are filing it as a non-adversarial information incident, satire misread as signal, with no coordinated actor.

The wider point is the failure mode. This sits alongside our fabricated-Bezos-quote report as a case of automated systems acting on information without the source-context judgement a human reader applies. Here the misreader was a trading system, and the consequence was financial.
