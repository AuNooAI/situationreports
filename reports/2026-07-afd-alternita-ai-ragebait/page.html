AfD "Alternita": a party-run AI system for mass-producing far-right "rage bait"

OSINT assessment from the Correctiv investigation (via The Irish Times) and Aunoo news-intelligence
Bottom line up front

Germany's far-right AfD has built and begun piloting an in-house content system called Alternita that turns far-right articles into ready-to-post "rage bait" for all major platforms in minutes. An undercover investigation by the German outlet Correctiv, reported by The Irish Times on 8 July 2026, documented it generating posts calling for forced deportations, framed as "remigration," and a post warning of an LGBT "rainbow dictatorship" with an AI-generated burning-flag image.

Correctiv examined the source code and found Alternita draws on three commercial AI engines: Google Gemini, OpenAI's ChatGPT, and Anthropic's Claude. The platform is registered to AfD general secretary Hans-Holger Malcomeß and is reportedly already running on co-leader Alice Weidel's channels, with not all output labeled as AI. Google and OpenAI said they are examining the use; Anthropic did not reply.

What is documented

Posing as a regional party member, a Correctiv journalist was given a tour of Alternita by Mario Hau, social-media chief of the AfD's Bundestag group. The tool automatically pulled in feeds from AfD-friendly outlets such as Nius. Given a test account, the journalist uploaded a Nius article on revoking citizenship, and the software produced posts demanding "consequential and involuntary remigration," meaning forced deportations, to prevent "ethnic elections." Left with the account, the reporters uploaded a neo-Nazi magazine article and generated a post warning of an LGBT "rainbow dictatorship" with an AI image of a burning rainbow flag. A linked account, a satirical fictional "federal minister for remigration" called Karl Ranseier, posts AI images including men boarding a train to a "deportation airport." Hau said the content foundation is the current party programme and that the tool is "tailor-made for the AfD."

Why it matters

Industrialized propaganda. Alternita lowers the time and skill to turn a partisan article into platform-ready, emotive content, produced centrally while the base still looks organic.
Provider-policy test. The tool runs on three mainstream models accessed as services. The open question is whether Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic can detect and stop such use, and how fast.
Labeling gap. Unlabeled synthetic political content on a party leader's channel runs against the AI Act's transparency rules and platform labeling efforts.
