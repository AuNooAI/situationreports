AfD "Alternita": A Party-Run AI System for Mass-Producing Far-Right "Rage Bait"

From the Correctiv investigation (via The Irish Times) and Aunoo news-intelligence
Bottom line up front

Germany's far-right Alternative for Germany (AfD) has built and begun piloting an in-house content system called Alternita that turns far-right articles into ready-to-post "rage bait" for all major social platforms in a few minutes. An undercover investigation by the German outlet Correctiv, reported by The Irish Times on 8 July 2026, documented the tool generating posts calling for forced deportations, framed as "remigration," and a post warning of an LGBT "rainbow dictatorship" with an AI-generated image of a burning rainbow flag.

Correctiv examined the source code and found Alternita draws on three commercial AI engines: Google Gemini, OpenAI's ChatGPT, and Anthropic's Claude. The platform is registered to AfD general secretary Hans-Holger Malcomeß and, per Correctiv, is already running on co-leader Alice Weidel's channels, with not all output labeled as AI-generated. Google and OpenAI said they are examining the use; Anthropic did not reply. It arrives before a September state-parliament election in an eastern state where the AfD is polling around 40 percent.

What is documented

Posing as an AfD regional party member, a Correctiv journalist was given an online tour of Alternita by Mario Hau, the social-media chief of the AfD's parliamentary group in the Bundestag. The version demonstrated automatically pulled in feeds from AfD-friendly far-right outlets, including the site Nius. Given a test account, the journalist uploaded a Nius article about revoking citizenship, and the software generated posts demanding "consequential and involuntary remigration," meaning forced deportations, to prevent "ethnic elections," tagged "#Homeland #Identity #That'sWhyAfD" in translation. Left alone with the account, the reporters uploaded a neo-Nazi magazine article and produced a post warning of an LGBT "rainbow dictatorship" with an AI image of a burning rainbow flag.

According to Correctiv, Alternita is already running on the channels of co-leader Alice Weidel, and not all content posted through it is flagged as AI-generated. A linked account, a satirical fictional "federal minister for remigration" called Karl Ranseier, reportedly operated by Hau and followed by Weidel, posts AI images including a "Ministry for Truth" and men boarding a train to a "deportation airport." The AfD says the software, launched in a pilot in the week of 6 July, will be ready for full implementation next year. Hau said the content foundation is the current party programme and that the tool is "tailor-made for the AfD." Correctiv examined the source code and found it draws on Google Gemini, OpenAI's ChatGPT, and Anthropic's Claude.

Why this matters

Industrialized propaganda. Alternita lowers the time and skill needed to turn a partisan article into platform-ready, emotive content, produced centrally while the base still looks organic.
Provider-policy test. The tool runs on three mainstream models accessed as services. The practical question is whether Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic can detect and stop such use, and how quickly. The exposure came from journalists, not the companies' own monitoring.
Labeling gap. Unlabeled synthetic political content on a party leader's channel runs against the AI Act's transparency obligations and platform labeling efforts.
Assessment

On the evidence reported, the existence of Alternita, its documented outputs, and its reliance on Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic models are well supported by Correctiv's direct demonstration and source-code analysis. The scale of current deployment is less certain, since the party calls it a pilot while Correctiv reports it is already active on Weidel's channels. Confidence: high on the tool's existence, capabilities, and AI engines; moderate on current live scale.
