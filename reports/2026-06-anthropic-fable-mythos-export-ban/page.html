US government switches off Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all foreign nationals

Flash assessment compiled from 16+ corroborating sources · Aunoo news-intelligence
Bottom line up front

On Friday 12 June 2026, the US Commerce Department ordered Anthropic to suspend its two most advanced AI models for every foreign national worldwide, citing national security. Unable to cleanly separate users, Anthropic disabled both models for everyone, US customers included. It is the first US export control ever aimed at a specific AI model rather than chips or hardware, and the first frontier release revoked just days after launch.

What happened

Anthropic released Fable 5 (public) and Mythos 5 (trusted partners only) on Tuesday 9 June. On 10–11 June, independent red-teamer "Pliny the Liberator" publicly jailbroke Fable 5, publishing working exploit code and a chemical-synthesis pathway and leaking the model's ~120,000-character system prompt. The government's order followed on 12 June. It cited a "jailbreak" as the concern but gave no specifics and did not name the public demonstration, so the link between the two is inferred rather than confirmed; the Wall Street Journal separately reports the trigger was talks between Amazon's CEO and US officials. All other Claude models remain available. Anthropic calls the order a "misunderstanding," says the flaw is minor and present in rival models too, and is working to restore access.

Why this is different

Scope of control. Earlier AI export controls targeted hardware: chips and compute. This directive targets the model itself.
Deployment risk. A public frontier release can now be reversed at short notice rather than over months.
Sovereignty pressure. Once one government can revoke access, others have cause to treat domestic model capability as strategic infrastructure.

The sovereignty signal is global

This is not a one-country story. The Financial Times framed the underlying dynamic as "the economics of AI scarcity," with the same thread running through The Economist, Forbes, and commentary across Asia and the Gulf. National "sovereign AI" debates — India's prominent among them — are all variations on one realization: when a frontier model can be switched off by another government, domestic capability stops being a preference and becomes infrastructure.
