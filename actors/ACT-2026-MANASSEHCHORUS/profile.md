## Bottom line up front (BLUF)

MANASSEH CHORUS is Aunoo's designation for the influence operation run by Brad Parscale's firm Clock Tower X on behalf of the State of Israel. TIME reported on 13 July 2026, from FARA filings it reviewed, that the agency Havas engaged Clock Tower X in September 2025 to produce and amplify pro-Israel content for young American conservatives at $1.5 million a month. The registered terms include at least 100 content pieces a month and 50 million monthly impressions, plus an explicit effort to shape how the major AI assistants characterize Israel and the war. The mechanism, per three people familiar with it, is a network of Parscale firms (Clock Tower X, Campaign Nucleus, Influenceable) that feed conservative influencers suggested language through private group chats and pay them by reach, producing what looks like an organic conversation.

Attribution to Parscale and to the Israeli sponsor is high, because the contract is FARA-registered and Parscale confirms the campaign. The profile is held at tentative and in-review because the core picture currently rests on a single principal investigation. The defining feature is structural: a disclosed, FARA-registered contract combined with allegedly undisclosed downstream amplification, a combination that is harder to categorize than either a covert network or a labeled advertisement.

The code name reflects the nexus and the method. MANASSEH, a notoriously wicked king of Judah, signals the Israel sponsorship in the house convention; CHORUS is the paid influencer network singing the same script in apparent independence.

## Attribution & confidence

**Diamond Model.**
- **Adversary:** Brad Parscale, operating through Clock Tower X and affiliated firms Campaign Nucleus and Influenceable. The commissioning sponsor is the State of Israel, engaged through the advertising agency Havas. Both the operator and the sponsor are documented in the FARA filing.
- **Capability:** A paid influencer "ecosystem" fed synchronized messaging; monthly content production at scale (100+ pieces); AI-optimized content sites built to be ingested by chatbots.
- **Infrastructure:** Private group chats used to distribute language to influencers; the interconnected firm network; the websites PaxPoint.org and FactSignal.org; and, per a draft Services Agreement, distribution through Salem Media Network properties (Parscale says his contract does not influence Salem hosts).
- **Victim / target:** Young American conservatives, and the outputs of commercial AI systems (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) as they describe Israel and the war.

**Confidence statement.** The existence, sponsor, price, and deliverables of the operation, including the AI-targeting clause, are assessed **high**, resting on TIME's review of the FARA filings and Parscale's on-record confirmation of the campaign. The reach and real-world effect of the influencer amplification are **moderate**, sourced to three anonymous people plus Clock Tower X's own marketing language and a prior visible example. The disputed claim that the operation worked against President Trump's ceasefire is **low** and denied by Parscale.

**Competing hypotheses.** (1) A contracted, disclosed public-diplomacy campaign whose downstream influencer amplification happens to be undisclosed — best fit with the FARA registration and Parscale's account. (2) A deliberately deniable astroturf operation using the registered contract as cover — supported by the private-group-chat mechanism and the March "soda industry" precedent. (3) A drifting operation that exceeded its brief and collided with the President's interests — asserted by U.S. officials, denied by Parscale. Discriminating evidence would be the influencer contracts and payment trail.

Load-bearing claims are graded **B2**: a high-factuality outlet working from primary FARA documents, with corroboration currently limited to one investigation.

## History & evolution

The operation grew out of an Israeli conclusion in 2025 that conventional public diplomacy (hasbara) was failing on the American right. In a May 2025 private letter to Netanyahu that TIME reviewed, the late activist Charlie Kirk warned that anti-Israel sentiment online had reached "record levels" and urged an aggressive influencer-led campaign. By the fall, Israel's Foreign Ministry had decided to hire an outside operator and engaged Clock Tower X through Havas.

## Targeting / victimology

The selection logic is audience-based rather than target-based: young American conservatives (Gen Z) on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and podcasts, chosen because Israel feared erosion of support on the right amid influence from figures such as Tucker Carlson and Steve Bannon and a Vance-aligned isolationism. A second, unusual target is the AI information layer: the operation seeks to influence how ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini describe Israel, and builds sites for those systems to ingest.

## Modus operandi

A disclosed, FARA-registered contract sits alongside a reported influencer-amplification network, and the relationship between the two funding streams is disputed. TIME reported, citing three people familiar with the campaign, that Parscale's firms produce pro-Israel content at volume, then distribute suggested language to conservative influencers through private group chats and compensate them by the reach they generate, so the messaging surfaces through voices the audience already trusts and reads as organic. Parscale says none of the FARA-registered money paid influencers. The operation also built AI-optimized websites intended to shape chatbot outputs, and, per the draft agreement, contemplated distribution through the Salem conservative-media ecosystem. The March 2026 "soda industry" episode, in which many MAGA accounts posted near-identical messages, is a visible instance of the same coordination pattern.
